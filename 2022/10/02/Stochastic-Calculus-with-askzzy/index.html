  1. The increments of the Poisson process are independent and follow a Poisson distribution with rate $λ(\Delta t)$;
  2. conditioned on the number of jumps occurred between $t_j$ and $t_{j+1}$, the sum of the jump severities is Gaussian with given mean and variance.

Hence, the simulation algorithm can be organized as follows.

  1. Simulate the continuous part of the JD diffusion process, i.e. the ABM, on the given time partition.

  2. Simulate the number of jumps occurring between $t_j$ and $t_{j+1}$, i.e. $N \sim Poi(λ(tj, tj+1))$.

  3. Generate $Z ∼ N (0, 1)$; set $J = μZ N + σZ \sqrt{N}Z$.
